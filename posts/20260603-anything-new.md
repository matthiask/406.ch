# Anything new?

A lot of time has passed since I officially announced that I want to [step down from maintaining django-mptt](https://406.ch/writing/weeknotes-2021-week-10/#django-mptt-is-not-maintained-anymore).

I already contributed to the library around 2009 since I used it in [FeinCMS](https://github.com/feincms/feincms) and, at least according to the git history, tagged the [0.3 release in April 2010](https://github.com/django-mptt/django-mptt/tree/0.3.0). Craig took over and led the project for many years. We even had additional maintainers for some time, but since somewhere in 2019 I have been the only remaining active maintainer.

The post about [django-tree-queries](https://406.ch/writing/django-tree-queries/) goes into more detail regarding django-mptt. That's not the topic of today's post anyway.


## The sense of entitlement

When a project is obviously unmaintained asking for free labor is walking a tightrope. It takes a lot of care to not rekindle the fires of frustration which did lead maintainers to abandoning the project in the first place.

Even though I can allocate a part of my work day towards Open Source, I still have to choose which project gets the time and attention.

There's a sense of accomplishment in a job well done. Abandoning a project isn't an easy choice for me, and probably for others as well. It's like stepping out of a relationship, where negative emotions are a somewhat necessary driver. The absence of positive events alone may not be sufficient reasons to step out.


## Clapping back or quitting quietly
